Gyros

Description

Traditional gyros, a beloved staple of Greek cuisine, offer a delicious blend of seasoned meat, fresh vegetables, and creamy tzatziki sauce, all wrapped in a warm pita. To prepare authentic gyros, start with the meatâ€”typically pork, chicken, or lamb. Marinate thinly sliced pieces in a mixture of olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, thyme, and a touch of red wine vinegar. Allow the meat to soak up the flavors for several hours, or preferably overnight.

Once marinated, cook the meat on a rotisserie or grill until it's perfectly tender and slightly crispy on the edges. While the meat cooks, prepare the accompaniments: slice ripe tomatoes, cucumbers, and red onions thinly. Warm the pita bread either on a grill or in the oven until it's soft and pliable. For the tzatziki sauce, combine Greek yogurt, grated cucumber, minced garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, and fresh dill, mixing until smooth and creamy.

To assemble the gyros, spread a generous amount of tzatziki on the warmed pita, add a handful of the cooked meat, and top with the sliced vegetables. Optionally, sprinkle with a few fries for added texture and flavor. Roll the pita around the fillings, securing with parchment paper or foil if desired. Serve immediately, enjoying the harmonious blend of savory meat, refreshing vegetables, and tangy sauce that makes gyros an enduring favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork, chicken, or lamb, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare the Marinade:
    • Prepare the Marinade: In a bowl, combine the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried thyme, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper.
    • Add the thinly sliced meat to the bowl, ensuring each piece is well coated with the marinade.
    • Cover and refrigerate for several hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to infuse the meat.
  2. Cook the Meat:
    • Preheat your grill or rotisserie.
    • Cook the marinated meat until it is tender and slightly crispy on the edges, ensuring it is cooked through. This may take about 15-20 minutes, depending on the thickness of the slices.
  3. Prepare the Tzatziki Sauce:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, grated cucumber (with excess water squeezed out), minced garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, fresh dill, salt, and pepper.
    • Stir until all ingredients are well blended and the sauce is smooth.
  4. Prepare the Vegetables:
    • Slice the tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion thinly and set aside.
  5. Warm the Pita Bread:
    • Warm the pita bread rounds either on the grill or in the oven until they are soft and pliable.
  6. Assemble the Gyros:
    • Spread a generous amount of tzatziki sauce on each warmed pita bread.
    • Add a handful of the cooked meat slices.
    • Top with the sliced tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion.
    • Optionally, add a few fries for extra texture and flavor.
    • Roll the pita around the fillings, securing with parchment paper or foil if desired.
  7. Serve:
    • Serve the gyros immediately and enjoy the delicious blend of savory meat, refreshing vegetables, and tangy tzatziki sauce.
